A sorting algorithm takes a list of items and orders them in a logical way. Any list with items that can be compared to each other using <, >, and = can be sorted. Sorting a list of items can help save time when searching for them in the future. For example, it is much easier to identify the smallest element when the list {17, 15, 14, 23, 28, 24, 19, 13, 26, 18} has been sorted into {13, 14, 15, 17, 18, 19, 23, 24, 26, 28}.

Typically, when sorting a list of integers, they are arranged either from smallest to largest or largest to smallest. Strings (lexicographically) and coordinates (by x value and then y value) are also commonly sorted items.
